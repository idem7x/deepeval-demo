# Spanish coastal and inland regions for property buyers

Spain is divided into 17 autonomous communities, and several of them have very
different property markets, climates, and legal nuances. The most popular
regions for foreign buyers are:

- **Costa del Sol** (Andalucía, Málaga province): year-round mild climate,
  large expat communities (Marbella, Estepona, Fuengirola). High demand,
  driving up prices, especially close to the coast.
- **Costa Blanca** (Comunitat Valenciana, Alicante province): cheaper than
  Costa del Sol, very popular with British, Dutch, and Scandinavian buyers.
  Key towns: Alicante, Torrevieja, Dénia, Jávea.
- **Costa Brava** (Catalonia, Girona province): rugged coastline north of
  Barcelona, more upscale and seasonal. Closer to France.
- **Balearic Islands** (Mallorca, Menorca, Ibiza, Formentera): premium
  segment. Mallorca alone accounts for a disproportionate share of
  foreign-buyer transactions in Spain.
- **Canary Islands** (Tenerife, Gran Canaria, Lanzarote, Fuerteventura):
  unique tax regime (lower IGIC instead of mainland IVA), year-round summer
  climate, popular for rentals.
- **Madrid**: the capital; strongest urban rental yields and the most liquid
  resale market in the country, but no sea.
- **Barcelona**: cosmopolitan, expensive, with strict short-term rental
  licensing (Llei 11/2009 and subsequent moratoriums) that materially
  affects investment returns.
- **Valencia city and L'Albufera**: balance of urban amenities, beach
  access, and prices noticeably below Madrid/Barcelona.

Regions matter for taxes too: the **ITP** (transfer tax on resale property)
is set by each autonomous community and ranges roughly from 6 % to 10 %.
Some regions (Madrid, Andalucía) have lowered it in recent years to attract
buyers; others (Catalonia, Comunitat Valenciana) sit at the higher end.
