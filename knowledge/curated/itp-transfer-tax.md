# ITP — Transfer tax on resale property

The **Impuesto sobre Transmisiones Patrimoniales (ITP)** is the tax you pay
when buying a **second-hand** (resale) residential property in Spain from a
private seller. It does **not** apply to new builds bought directly from the
developer — those carry **IVA** (10 % for residential) plus **AJD**
(Actos Jurídicos Documentados, roughly 0.5 % – 1.5 % depending on region).

## Rate

ITP is set by each autonomous community and applies to the reference value
or declared price (whichever is higher under the "valor de referencia"
system in force since 2022). Indicative rates for residential property
(may change — verify before buying):

| Region | Standard rate |
|---|---|
| Andalucía | 7 % |
| Comunitat Valenciana | 10 % |
| Catalonia | 10 % (11 % above €1 M) |
| Madrid | 6 % |
| Murcia | 8 % |
| Balearic Islands | 8 %–13 % (progressive) |
| Canary Islands | 6.5 % |

Many regions offer reductions (e.g. for under-35 buyers, large families, or
properties in rural depopulated areas — *zonas de despoblación*).

## When and how it's paid

You must self-assess and pay ITP within **30 working days** of signing the
*escritura pública de compraventa* before a notary. The tax is paid to the
**autonomous community**, not the central government, using its specific
form (Andalucía form 600, Catalonia form 600, etc.). The Property Registry
will not register the deed in your name until the tax is paid.

## Common pitfall: valor de referencia

Since 2022, ITP is calculated on the **higher** of (a) the declared sale
price and (b) the *valor de referencia catastral* published by the
Catastro. If you buy below the valor de referencia, you still pay ITP on
the higher amount — and you may also face an unexpected
*ganancia patrimonial* on your IRPF later if you sell.
