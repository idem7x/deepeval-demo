# The buying process step by step

A typical purchase of a resale property in Spain by a foreign buyer takes
**6 – 10 weeks** from accepted offer to keys.

## 1. Pre-checks (before you offer)

- Obtain a **nota simple** from the *Registro de la Propiedad* — it lists
  the current legal owner, the cadastral reference, any *cargas*
  (mortgages, embargoes, *afecciones fiscales*), and the boundaries.
- Verify the **IBI receipt** is up to date and matches the cadastral
  reference.
- Check the **community of owners (*comunidad de propietarios*)**:
  request a *certificado de estar al corriente* — confirming no unpaid
  community fees.

## 2. Contrato de arras (deposit contract)

- A private contract where the buyer typically pays **10 %** of the
  purchase price.
- Usually structured as **arras penitenciales** (Civil Code art. 1454):
  if the buyer backs out, they lose the deposit; if the seller backs
  out, they pay back **double**.
- It locks the price and removes the property from the market.

## 3. NIE + bank account + funds

- Get the **NIE** if you don't have one (see the NIE document).
- Open a Spanish bank account (usually a *cuenta no residente* until you
  formalise residency).
- Transfer purchase funds to that account; the bank will need to file an
  **anti-money-laundering declaration (S1)** for amounts over €100,000.

## 4. Mortgage (if applicable)

- Typical max LTV for non-residents: **60–70 %** of the
  *valor de tasación* (bank's appraisal).
- Mortgage formal offer (**FEIN**) must be issued at least **10 calendar
  days** before signing the deed (Ley 5/2019 cooling-off period).

## 5. Escritura pública de compraventa

- Both parties sign the **public deed of sale** before a *notario*.
- Buyer pays the remaining price (typically by bank-certified cheque,
  *cheque bancario*), receives the keys, and gets a *copia simple* of
  the deed.

## 6. Taxes and registry

- Pay **ITP** (or IVA + AJD for new builds) within **30 working days**.
- Lodge the deed with the **Registro de la Propiedad**; registration
  typically takes 2 – 8 weeks.
- Notify the **Catastro** of the change of ownership (Form 901).

## Typical costs on top of the purchase price

Rule of thumb: budget **10 – 14 %** of the purchase price for
acquisition costs (ITP/IVA, notary, registry, lawyer, gestoría,
mortgage taxes if any).
