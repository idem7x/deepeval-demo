# Mortgages for non-resident foreign buyers

Spanish banks are open to lending to non-residents, but on **stricter
terms** than to residents. Plan for this when budgeting.

## Typical LTV (loan-to-value)

| Borrower profile | Typical max LTV |
|---|---|
| Spanish tax resident | up to 80 % |
| EU non-resident | 60 – 70 % |
| Non-EU non-resident (UK post-Brexit, US, CH, etc.) | 50 – 60 % |

LTV is computed against the **lower** of the purchase price and the
bank's appraisal (*valor de tasación*), not the higher.

## Rate types

- **Fijo** (fixed-rate, full term) — most common for new mortgages since
  2022. Typical term 20 – 30 years.
- **Variable** (Euribor + differential). The reference rate, recalculated
  every 6 or 12 months, is the **12-month Euribor**.
- **Mixto** — fixed for the first 5 – 10 years, then variable.

Differentials for non-residents typically run **0.50 – 1.00 pp higher**
than for residents.

## Required documents

- NIE certificate (the number, not residency).
- Last **2 years of tax returns** in your country of residence.
- Last **6 months of payslips** (or self-employed equivalent — accounts,
  tax filings).
- Last **6 months of bank statements** for all accounts.
- Existing-debt schedule (other mortgages, credit cards, car loans).
- Credit report (e.g. Experian/Equifax for UK; SCHUFA for Germany).

Banks usually want the borrower's **total monthly debt service**
(including the new Spanish mortgage) to stay below **35 – 40 %** of net
income.

## Costs

Since the **Ley 5/2019 reform**, almost all set-up costs are paid by the
lender, with two exceptions still on the borrower:

- **Tasación** (appraisal): €300 – €600, depending on property value.
- **Copia de la escritura**: small notary copy fee.

## FEIN — mandatory cooling-off

The **Ficha Europea de Información Normalizada (FEIN)** is the binding
mortgage offer. By law you must receive it at least **10 calendar days**
before signing the mortgage deed, and you must visit a notary **once
during that window** to acknowledge that you understand the terms (this
visit is free). Without this acknowledgement, the deed cannot be signed.
