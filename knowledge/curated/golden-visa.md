# Spain Golden Visa — abolished as of 3 April 2025

The Spanish **Golden Visa** for investors, created by Ley 14/2013, allowed
non-EU citizens to obtain a residence permit by making certain qualifying
investments — most prominently a **€500,000 real estate purchase** with no
mortgage on that amount. After more than a decade in force, the **real
estate path was abolished**.

## What changed

- The Spanish government announced the abolition of the real estate route
  on **8 April 2024**, citing housing affordability concerns in major
  cities (Madrid, Barcelona, Málaga, Balearic Islands).
- It was formally repealed via the **Ley Orgánica 1/2025**, with the
  abolition taking effect on **3 April 2025**.
- Applications submitted **before** 3 April 2025 are processed under the
  old rules. Renewals of permits already granted continue to be possible.

## What still exists

The Golden Visa law repealed only the *real estate* and certain
*financial* investment paths. As of 2025–2026, the following alternatives
remain or have appeared:

- **Digital Nomad Visa** (Ley 28/2022 de Startups): for remote workers
  with foreign employers/clients, minimum income roughly
  €2,762/month (200 % of *Salario Mínimo Interprofesional*, recalculated
  annually). Includes a favourable **Beckham-like tax regime** capped at
  24 % up to €600,000.
- **Non-Lucrative Visa**: for those with passive income, no work in Spain
  allowed. Requires showing roughly €30,000/year of passive income for
  the main applicant (400 % of IPREM), plus 100 % per dependant.
- **Entrepreneur Visa**: for innovative business projects assessed by
  ENISA.

## Implication for foreign buyers

Buying property in Spain still grants no residency rights by itself, and
no longer grants a path through the Golden Visa. UK, US, and other
non-EU buyers must rely on the Schengen 90/180-day rule for visits, or
apply for one of the visa types above on independent grounds.
