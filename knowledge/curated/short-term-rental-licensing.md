# Short-term tourist rentals (VFT / VUT) — licensing

If you intend to rent your Spanish property short-term to tourists, you
need a **regional tourist licence**, and in many cities **also a
municipal one**. Rules are aggressive and have tightened sharply since
2022.

## Two layers of regulation

1. **Autonomous community licence** — called *Vivienda de Uso Turístico
   (VUT)*, *Vivienda con Fines Turísticos (VFT)*, *Habitatge d'Ús
   Turístic (HUT)*, etc. depending on region. Issued by the regional
   tourism authority and yields a registration number that **must
   appear in every listing** (Airbnb, Booking, Vrbo).
2. **Municipal licence and zoning** — many city councils restrict the
   total number of VUTs per neighbourhood or per building. Issued (or
   denied) by the *ayuntamiento*.

## Key cities to know

- **Barcelona**: a **moratorium on new HUT licences** has been in force
  since 2014. The number is **frozen at ~9,600** and the city has
  announced its intention to **terminate all HUT licences by 2028**.
  Effectively, you cannot start a new short-term rental in Barcelona
  today.
- **Madrid**: a 2019 *Plan Especial de Hospedaje* requires VUTs in the
  central *almendra* to have an **independent street entrance**,
  effectively excluding apartments inside residential buildings.
- **Palma de Mallorca**: a 2018 ban prohibits **all VUTs in
  apartments** (single-family houses still allowed in some zones).
- **Málaga**: from 2024, a moratorium on new VUTs in central districts
  with VUT density above a threshold.
- **Canary Islands**: a new VV (*vivienda vacacional*) law passed in
  2024 requires the property to be in a *zoned* tourist area and
  introduces minimum quality standards.

## Community of owners

Since the **2019 reform of the LPH** (*Ley de Propiedad Horizontal*),
the community of owners can prohibit short-term tourist rentals with a
**3/5 (60 %) majority** vote. Many newly built residential blocks now
explicitly forbid VUT use in their statutes.

## Tax treatment

Short-term tourist rental income is **regular rental income** for IRNR
purposes (Form 210, quarterly). The Modelo 179 information return,
filed by the intermediation platforms (Airbnb, Booking), means Hacienda
has direct visibility into your bookings.
