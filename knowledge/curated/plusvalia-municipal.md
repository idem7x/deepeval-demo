# Plusvalía municipal

The **Impuesto sobre el Incremento de Valor de los Terrenos de Naturaleza
Urbana (IIVTNU)**, commonly called *plusvalía municipal*, is a municipal
tax on the **increase in the value of the urban land** during the years
the seller owned it.

It is **paid by the seller** (in resale transactions). Buyers care
because, when the seller is non-resident, the buyer becomes a
**substitutional taxpayer (*sustituto del contribuyente*)** under Spanish
law — meaning Hacienda will come to **you** if the seller doesn't pay.
This is why buyers' lawyers routinely withhold the estimated plusvalía
from the price paid at the deed and pay it directly to the *ayuntamiento*
on the seller's behalf.

## How it's calculated

Since the **2021 Constitutional Court ruling (STC 182/2021)** that struck
down the old formula, sellers can choose the **lower** of two methods:

1. **Objective method**: cadastral land value × a coefficient (set by the
   municipality, depending on years of ownership) × the municipal rate
   (up to 30 %).
2. **Real method**: actual gain on the land = (sale price − purchase
   price), apportioned to the land share of the cadastral value, taxed
   at the municipal rate.

If the seller can prove there was **no increase** in land value
(e.g., sold at a loss compared to acquisition), **no plusvalía is owed**.
This requires comparing both deeds and is asserted via *autoliquidación
con resultado a ingresar cero* and supporting documentation.

## Deadlines

- **30 working days** from the deed signing for the seller to file and
  pay (some municipalities use *liquidación* — they send you the bill).
- Late filing carries surcharges (5 %, 10 %, 15 %, 20 % depending on
  delay) plus interest.

## Practical workflow

A diligent buyer's *gestoría* will:

1. Estimate plusvalía using both methods before signing.
2. Withhold the higher of the two estimates from the seller at the deed.
3. File and pay within 30 days using the seller's NIE.
4. Refund any over-withholding to the seller once the *ayuntamiento*
   confirms the final amount.
