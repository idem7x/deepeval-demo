# IRNR — Income tax for non-resident property owners

The **Impuesto sobre la Renta de no Residentes (IRNR)** is the Spanish
income tax owed by **non-residents** who own property in Spain. It applies
even if you never rent the property out — Spain imputes a deemed income
just for ownership.

## Two scenarios

### 1. Property used only by the owner (no rental income)

Spain imputes a notional income of:

- **1.1 %** of the *valor catastral* if it was revised in the last 10 years,
- **2 %** otherwise.

That imputed income is taxed at:

- **19 %** for residents of the EU, EEA (Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein),
- **24 %** for everyone else (UK after Brexit, US, Switzerland, etc.).

You file **Form 210** annually, due by **31 December** of the year
**following** the tax year.

### 2. Property rented out

You pay tax on the **rental income** (gross minus deductible expenses for
EU/EEA residents; **gross only** for non-EU residents, who **cannot
deduct** expenses at all). Same rates apply: 19 % EU/EEA, 24 % others.

Form 210 must be filed **quarterly** (January, April, July, October) for
each property under rent.

## Deductible expenses (EU/EEA residents only)

- Mortgage interest (not principal)
- IBI and waste/garbage tax (*tasa de basuras*)
- Community fees, insurance, repairs, depreciation
- Property management fees

Non-EU residents pay 24 % on **gross** rent — this is materially worse
and is the main tax reason UK buyers post-Brexit consider routing
ownership through a Spanish company (SL).

## Common mistake

Many non-resident owners forget the **imputed income** filing during years
the property sits empty. Hacienda actively cross-checks Property Registry
data against IRNR filings, and back-tax + interest claims for missing
Form 210 filings are common.
