# NIE — Foreigner identification number

The **NIE** is a personal tax/identification number that Spain assigns to
foreigners. **You cannot buy property, open a Spanish bank account, take
out a mortgage, or pay any Spanish tax without one.** It is the single
hardest prerequisite for a foreign buyer to obtain in time.

## Where it's issued

Three channels:

1. **In Spain**, at a *Comisaría de Policía Nacional* with a
   *Oficina de Extranjería*. You need an appointment via the
   *sede.administracionespublicas.gob.es* portal. Appointments in
   high-demand cities (Madrid, Barcelona, Málaga, Alicante) can be booked
   out for weeks.
2. **At a Spanish consulate** in your country of residence (the typical
   path for buyers who haven't visited Spain yet).
3. **By power of attorney (*poder notarial*)** granted to a Spanish
   lawyer, who applies on your behalf in Spain. This is the fastest path
   if you can sign the POA at a Spanish embassy or have it apostilled.

## Documents required

- Form **EX-15** (application for an NIE).
- Form **790 código 012** plus payment receipt (currently around €9.84).
- Original passport + photocopy.
- Justification of *causa económica, profesional o social* — usually a
  signed *contrato de arras* (deposit contract) or a notarised statement
  of intent to buy a specific property.

## What you receive

A piece of A4 paper (the *certificado de NIE*) with your name, passport
number, and a number that starts with a letter (X, Y, or Z), followed by
seven digits, ending in a check letter — e.g. `Y1234567B`. **It is not a
physical ID card**, and you should keep multiple originals because
notaries and tax offices each want to retain a copy.

The NIE itself does **not** expire as a number, but the certificate
documents have shown an expiry date in recent years (3 months); the
underlying number remains yours forever.
